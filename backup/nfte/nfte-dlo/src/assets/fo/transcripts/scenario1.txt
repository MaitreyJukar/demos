Do you play video games? If so, you’re not alone. In fact, there is someone who plays video games regularly in nearly two-thirds of American homes. 

Since gaming devices became popular in the late 1980s, games could be played on a handheld device or a console-based system. 

Although some games were produced for both formats, there was no way to move between the two types of systems while playing a single game. This was the “usual” in the gaming console market until someone challenged it.
